  1. Dholavira

    2. Dholavira is an archaeological site at Khadirbet in Bhachau Taluka of Kutch
    District, in the state of Gujarat, western India, which is named after a modern-day
    village 1 kilometre (0.62 mi) south of it. This village is 165 km (103 mi) from Radhanpur.
    Also known locally as Kotada timba, the site contains ruins of a city of the ancient Indus Valley Civilization

  2. Bhuj

    3. Bhuj city and the Kutch region were established by the Maharaja of the Parmar Rajputs, and deterred invasions for centuries.
    Bhuj city was occupied by Rao Hamir in 1510 and made the capital of Kutch (kachchh) by Rao Khengarji I in 1549

  3. unagadh

    4. Junagadh is located at the foot of the Girnar Hills in southwestern Gujarat, approximately 355 km southwest of Ahmedabad and Gandhinagar,
    the state capital. The city lies on the Kathiawar Peninsula, near the Arabian Sea, and features a mix of plains and forested hills, including the Gir
    Hills, which are home to wildlife sanctuaries and the Asiatic lion
